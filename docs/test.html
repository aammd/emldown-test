Winter and summer comparison of biological, chemical, and physical conditions in seasonally ice-covered lakes

Data table(s) 1

Other entity(ies) 1

Abstract

The goal of this data collection was to compile diverse existing data in order to summarize and contrast ecologically relevant parameters in lakes during ice-on (winter) and ice-free (summer stratification) periods. There are 89 unique lakes in this dataset, ranging from small, shallow lakes to large, deep lakes. Lakes are from eleven different countries, primarily in North America and Europe; Antarctica is the only southern hemisphere location represented in the dataset. Variables collected include physical, chemical, and biological, as well as a suite of metadata variables. Chlorophyll a data are particularly well-represented across lakes, while many variables (e.g. zooplankton variables) are only available for a smaller subset of lakes. Temporal coverage for unique lake/sample stations range from one year to a span of 73 years, with 20 lake/stations having at least 30 years of data. Synthesis analyses based on this dataset are available in: Hampton et al. (2016). Ecology under lake ice. Ecology Letters. doi: 10.1111/ele.12699. It must be stressed that the data here are not raw values from the original sampling, but aggregated values, based on varying initial sampling efforts and protocols, calculated by individual researchers. Please carefully review Supplemental Document S1 and the methods described in Hampton et al. (2016) for detailed information about how data were collected and aggregated, as well as units associated with each variable.

Intellectual Rights

This dataset provides biological, chemical, and physical data for a wide array of lakes, with extensive spatial and temporal coverage. Synthesis analyses are available for review in: Hampton et al. (2016). Ecology under lake ice. Ecology Letters. doi: 10.1111/ele.12699. This dataset is available for further analysis. Please consider contacting one or all of the contacts if you are interested in collaborating on related or new topics with this dataset. Please note that 13 datasets included in Hampton et al. 2016 are not able to be publicly published at this time. These include one long-term dataset from Lake Baikal and short-term datasets for 12 lakes in Minnesota (Bellevue, Blakesly, Frolund, Leverson, Mavis West, Morrison, Murk, Org, Pisa, Skunk, Todd North, and Todd South). For information about the 12 Minnesota lakes, please contact Leah Domine (domi7653@stthomas.edu). For copies of data aggregation instructions and format template submitted to project participants, see Supplemental Information (Doc S1) for Hampton et al. 2016. Please note that the data here is provided "as is" and future users may need to filter and otherwise subset the data to suit their needs. For example, there are zero values in this data set for variables which may in fact simply be below detection limits, as well as non-zero values that may be below most modern detection limits (e.g. TDP < 1 ug/L). Users are encouraged to use their expert judgement in cases of potential outliers. Additionally, a careful reading of the information provided by researchers in the "comments" and "narrat" fields for each sample/row in this dataset will facilitate decision-making about which data subsets are most appropriate, depending on research question. For instance, net size may have consequences for zooplankton proportions (i.e. rotifers) and labratory methodology may impact results (e.g. dissolved nutrients). For specific information about the data subset used in Hampton et al. 2016, please see the analysis scripts on Github (DOI: 10.5281/zenodo.179248) or contact Stephanie Hampton.
